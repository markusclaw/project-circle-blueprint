Executive Summary

Project Circle is a trust-centered introduction platform built around self-knowledge, intentionality, and socially grounded human connection. It is not a dating app — it is relationship infrastructure.

Core Thesis: People are exhausted by algorithmic attention economies disguised as connection tools. Circle inverts that model by making the primary output self-understanding, with meaningful connection as a byproduct of intentional self-knowledge.

What Circle Does

The platform helps users understand themselves deeply through a five-layer personality assessment (MBTI, Big Five, Attachment Theory, Communication Style, and Dynamic Behavioral Learning), then uses that understanding to facilitate curated, trust-grounded introductions within socially dense communities.

Why Now

The $9B+ dating app market is structurally misaligned with users. More swiping equals more revenue. More churn equals more subscriptions. More emotional volatility equals more engagement. Circle aligns incentives differently — the platform succeeds when users form real relationships and leave the app.

The Moat

The competitive advantage is not primarily the LLM. The long-term moat becomes trusted identity systems, dense trusting networks, longitudinal behavioral learning, compatibility feedback loops, and emotional trust as structural advantage. Competitors starting from scratch cannot replicate compounding personality data.

Design Philosophy

The product should feel calm (opposite of frantic swiping), intentional (every interaction meaningful), safe (identity trusted, behavior moderated), human (emotionally intelligent, warm), and premium (sophisticated, not cluttered). The AI is infrastructural, not theatrical — it should feel like being understood, not profiled.

02

Core Product Principles

These principles are non-negotiable. They define every product, design, and engineering decision.

1. Trust Over Engagement

Circle optimizes for trust and real outcomes, not time-on-app. No infinite swiping, no slot-machine mechanics, no boosts, no addiction loops, no psychological manipulation.

2. Self-Knowledge First

The primary output is self-understanding. Connection is a byproduct. Users gain value even without matches. Personality insights are a product unto themselves. Onboarding should feel reflective, not diagnostic.

3. Human-Centered AI

AI should be invisible and warm. Users feel "I feel understood" — not "I am being analyzed." Conversational tone, not clinical. All AI conclusions visible and overridable by users.

4. Real-World Connection

Digital interaction is transitional. The app exists to create healthier real-world relationships. Circle succeeds when users meet in person, form bonds, and eventually leave the app.

5. Safety Is Infrastructure

Safety is not a backend feature. It is visual language, interaction pacing, identity verification, moderation, and emotional atmosphere. Safety ships in Phase 1, not Phase 5.

6. Intentionality Over Scale

Launch deep, not broad. Network density matters more than raw user count. Trust accumulates in small, high-context communities. Growth follows trust, not vice versa.

Incentive Alignment: Circle succeeds when introductions feel meaningful, users trust the platform deeply, users form real relationships, users invite others and communities strengthen, and successful users reduce time-on-app because they're living.

▶ Layer 1: MBTI (UX & Communication Layer) Familiarity + Shareability

Purpose: Familiarity, shareability, conversational framing. People understand and love MBTI — it's culturally recognizable. It is NOT the scientific backbone, but the user-facing personality language.

Implementation: Derived from Big Five scores, not assessed separately. Displayed prominently on user profiles as a recognizable anchor point.

▶ Layer 2: Big Five / OCEAN (Scientific Engine) Primary Foundation

Purpose: The most stable, validated personality framework in psychology. Directly maps to relationship compatibility research. This is the backbone of all matching logic.

  • Openness: Curiosity, creativity, willingness to try new things
  • Conscientiousness: Organization, discipline, responsibility
  • Extraversion: Sociability, energy, social dominance
  • Agreeableness: Compassion, cooperation, trust
  • Neuroticism (Emotional Stability): Anxiety, sensitivity, emotional regulation

Each dimension scored 0-100 via Claude-powered conversational assessment. Scores are transparent to users and editable.

▶ Layer 3: Attachment Theory (Relationship Health) Most Predictive Layer

Purpose: Core relationship health predictor. Enormously predictive of relationship outcomes — more important than any single other variable.

  • Secure: Confident, communicative, trusting
  • Anxious: Seeks reassurance, fears abandonment
  • Avoidant: Values independence, uncomfortable with intimacy
  • Fearful-Avoidant: Conflicted approach-avoidance

Circle helps users understand their attachment style without clinical language, framing it as growth potential rather than diagnosis.

▶ Layer 4: Communication & Relationship Style Daily Life Signals

Purpose: Practical compatibility signals for everyday life together.

  • Love Languages: Words, acts, time, touch, gifts
  • Communication style: Direct/indirect, emotional/rational
  • Conflict style: Avoidant, confrontational, collaborative
  • Emotional pacing: Fast/slow emotional escalation preferences
▶ Layer 5: Dynamic Behavioral Learning (Long-Term Moat) Compounding Advantage

Purpose: The real moat. Matching improves with every user interaction. Competitors starting from scratch can never catch up.

  • Longitudinal learning from conversations
  • AI builds vector embeddings of personality from interactions
  • Feedback loops from real-world meeting outcomes
  • Post-intro satisfaction signals refine matching
  • 6-month and 1-year relationship success tracking

Concierge MVP Model

This is the most important strategic decision in v4. The first 100-300 introductions are NOT algorithmically automated. They are human-curated.

This follows the proven startup principle: do things that do not scale first. Airbnb manually photographed listings, Uber manually dispatched rides. Circle manually curates introductions first. The purpose is not efficiency — the purpose is learning.

What Concierge Validates

  1. Users complete self-knowledge onboarding
  2. Users trust enough to share deeply
  3. Curated intros feel better than swiping
  4. Social proximity increases trust
  5. Users prefer intentional matching over infinite choice
  6. Users return because they feel understood
  7. Emotional pacing feels healthy
  8. Real-world meetings actually happen

Database Schema

Core schema for Supabase PostgreSQL with pgvector extension. All personality/psychology fields encrypted at rest via column-level encryption.

-- Core user table CREATE TABLE users ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(), email TEXT UNIQUE NOT NULL, phone TEXT, display_name TEXT NOT NULL, avatar_url TEXT, identity_verified BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE, trust_score INTEGER DEFAULT 0, intent_mode TEXT DEFAULT 'dating', -- dating | friendship | community | self-discovery status TEXT DEFAULT 'onboarding', -- onboarding | active | paused | deactivated created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now(), updated_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now() );
-- Personality assessment results (ENCRYPTED) CREATE TABLE assessments ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(), user_id UUID REFERENCES users(id) ON DELETE CASCADE, version INTEGER DEFAULT 1, mbti_type TEXT, -- e.g. 'ENFJ' big_five JSONB, -- {openness: 78, conscientiousness: 65, ...} attachment TEXT, -- secure | anxious | avoidant | fearful comm_style JSONB, -- {love_lang: 'quality_time', conflict: 'collaborative', ...} raw_responses JSONB, -- encrypted: full Q&A from Claude interview claude_analysis TEXT, -- encrypted: Claude's narrative interpretation user_overrides JSONB, -- any user corrections to AI conclusions created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now(), CONSTRAINT uq_user_version UNIQUE(user_id, version) );
-- Personality embedding vectors for similarity matching CREATE TABLE embeddings ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(), user_id UUID REFERENCES users(id) ON DELETE CASCADE UNIQUE, vector VECTOR(1536), -- personality embedding from Claude analysis model_version TEXT, -- embedding model version for migration updated_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now() ); CREATE INDEX idx_embeddings_vector ON embeddings USING ivfflat (vector vector_cosine_ops);
-- Match proposals (curator or algorithm generated) CREATE TABLE matches ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(), user_a UUID REFERENCES users(id), user_b UUID REFERENCES users(id), similarity FLOAT, -- cosine similarity score curator_id UUID, -- NULL if algorithmic reasoning TEXT, -- why they were matched (shown to users) status TEXT DEFAULT 'proposed', -- proposed | a_accepted | b_accepted | mutual | declined | expired quality_score FLOAT, -- post-intro feedback score created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now() );
-- Conversations between matched users CREATE TABLE conversations ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(), match_id UUID REFERENCES matches(id), created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now() ); CREATE TABLE messages ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(), conversation_id UUID REFERENCES conversations(id), sender_id UUID REFERENCES users(id), content TEXT, -- encrypted at rest created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now() );
-- Post-intro feedback loop CREATE TABLE feedback ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(), match_id UUID REFERENCES matches(id), user_id UUID REFERENCES users(id), connected BOOLEAN, -- did they connect? met_irl BOOLEAN, -- did they meet in person? quality INTEGER CHECK(quality BETWEEN 1 AND 5), notes TEXT, created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now() );
▶ Additional Tables (moderation, audit, billing)
-- Safety & moderation CREATE TABLE reports ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY, reporter_id UUID, reported_id UUID, reason TEXT, description TEXT, status TEXT DEFAULT 'pending', resolved_by UUID, resolved_at TIMESTAMPTZ, created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now() ); -- Audit log for data access (GDPR compliance) CREATE TABLE audit_log ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY, actor_id UUID, action TEXT, resource_type TEXT, resource_id UUID, metadata JSONB, created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now() ); -- Subscriptions & billing CREATE TABLE subscriptions ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY, user_id UUID REFERENCES users(id), stripe_subscription_id TEXT UNIQUE, stripe_customer_id TEXT, plan TEXT, status TEXT, current_period_end TIMESTAMPTZ, created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now() ); -- Fairness audit results CREATE TABLE fairness_audits ( id UUID PRIMARY KEY, audit_date DATE, metrics JSONB, findings TEXT, action_taken TEXT, auditor TEXT, created_at TIMESTAMPTZ DEFAULT now() );

Privacy Architecture

Circle handles extremely sensitive data: psychological traits, identity verification, social graph relationships, romantic intent, conversations, and emotional patterns. Privacy is foundational, not optional.

Mandatory Privacy Principles

Data Minimization

Collect only necessary data. No passive behavioral tracking beyond what's needed for matching.

Separate Sensitive Stores

Identity verification data NEVER lives beside personality profile data. Separate systems, separate access boundaries.

Explicit Consent

Users understand what is stored, processed, visible, and what affects matching. Two consent checkpoints during onboarding.

No Silent AI Training

User data is NOT used to train models by default. Ever. Opt-in only for research participation.

Full Deletion Rights

Export profile (JSON/CSV), delete all data, revoke consent, disconnect cleanly. 7-day grace period, 30-day complete removal.

Encrypted Everything

AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit. Column-level encryption for psychology fields. Key rotation quarterly via AWS KMS.

GDPR & CCPA Compliance

▶ Full compliance details (consent flows, deletion procedures, data portability)

GDPR

  • Right to Access: Users export full profile including AI assessments
  • Right to Deletion: Full data deletion within 30 days (7-day grace period)
  • Right to Portability: Export in standardized JSON/CSV format
  • Data Processing Agreement: Clear Privacy Policy before onboarding

CCPA

  • Consumer Rights: Know what's collected, delete personal information, opt-out of sale/sharing
  • Non-discrimination: Users cannot be charged more for opting out
  • Response timeline: 45 days for consumer requests
  • Annual privacy audit by external counsel

Psychology Data Sensitivity

Personality assessments are treated as Protected Health Information (PHI) equivalent. Encrypted database, authorized staff only, audit logs on all access, regular penetration testing, breach notification within 30 days.

Incident Response Playbooks

▶ Data Breach Response Protocol Critical
  1. Discovery (Immediate): Isolate affected systems, assemble incident response team, assess scope
  2. Internal notification (<2 hours): CEO, legal counsel, compliance officer
  3. Legal assessment (<24 hours): Determine which regulations apply, prepare notifications
  4. User notification (<72 hours): Email + in-app + SMS with breach details, impact, remediation steps
  5. Public statement (<7 days): If significant, transparent disclosure to media
  6. Post-incident review (<30 days): Root cause analysis, lessons learned, security improvements
▶ AI Bias Detection & Response
  1. Monthly automated audit: Alert if any demographic group has >20% match acceptance disparity
  2. Fairness audit (<48h): External auditor analyzes matching outcomes across age, gender, race, orientation
  3. Hotfix vs rollback (<1 week): Minor bias = prompt fix; significant bias = immediate rollback
  4. User communication (<2 weeks): Transparency report on findings and corrections
  5. Prevention: Bias checks in CI/CD pipeline, fairness sign-off before prompt changes deploy
